Methodology Note

REACH Syria Agricultural Analysis App

Overview

This document outlines the methodology behind the datasets and UI of the agricultural analysis web-application. The app displays three datasets: NDVI composites, surface water and precipitation. The details of each dataset is described below.

 

NDVI Composites

Source data: Sentinel-2

Script: NDVI Composites

  1. Cloud masking. Each image in the Sentinel-2 time-series was cloud and cloud shadow masked using the Sen2CloudLess algorithm: https://developers.google.com/earth-engine/tutorials/community/sentinel-2-s2cloudless.
  2. Mosaicking/compositing at monthly intervals. This process returned one image per month, with each pixel representing the mean value of all non-masked pixels available within the given month.
  3. Masking out non-cropland. The mosaics were masked using the FAO WAPOR land cover dataset to show only agricultural areas.

The cloud free coverage of the mosaics was 85-99%, with the vast majority reaching >95% coverage.

 

Surface Water Classifications

Source data: Sentinel-1 and -2, USRG SRTM (DEM), Merit Hydro (HAND), GLAD (https://glad.umd.edu/dataset/global-surface-water-dynamics)

Script (Training): https://code.earthengine.google.com/86b72ae7e9ade9165ac3a36d9c7ba8a7

Script (Classification): https://code.earthengine.google.com/d7a3b5086260238b1f99491aa9b307cf

 

The Surface Water maps were generated through supervised image classification using training data and the Random Forest (RF) classifier.  All processing was performed in Google Earth Engine (GEE).

  1. Generating training data. Monthly surface water datasets at 30 m resolution were available from GLAD 1999-2020. The GLAD surface water maps were used as sampling areas after applying a negative buffer of 30 m. 200 water and 800 land points were randomly sampled from each monthly map.
  2. Preparing a layer stack. The layer-stack included the following:
    • Cloud masked Sentinel-2 composites process similarly to the NDVI composites (bands: MNDWI, NDVI, blue, green, red, nir, swir1, swir2). The Sentinel-2 time-series was snow masking using a combination of automatic spectral based thresholds and manual delineation.
    • Sentinel-1 monthly composites (descending and ascending, bands: VV, VH).
    • Elevation
    • Slope
    • HAND (Height Above Nearest Drainage)
  3. Training the model. The RF was trained on the training points generated in Step 1 , with associated features (pixel values extracted from the layer stack) from the corresponding month. I.e. training points from a given month were associate with satellite observations from the same month. Subsequently, all features extracted from one year were combined into one training dataset. Thereby, the RF was trained on data from one full calendar year.
  4. Classifying. The monthly surface water maps were classified in annual batches, with the RF model trained on data for from the corresponding year. The RF model for the 2021 classification was trained on 2020 data.
  5. Iteration. The training was augmented based on the classification output, and the classifications rerun until a satisfactory result was achieved.
  6. Validation. The surface water classifications were validated visually. Attempts were made to calculate accuracies based on a subset of the training data. However, biases induced through the training data sampling technique resulted in unreliably high accuracies. A proper validation would require manual independently sampled data points.
